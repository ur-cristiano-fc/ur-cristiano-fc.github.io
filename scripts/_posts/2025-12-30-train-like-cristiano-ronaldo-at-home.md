## Your Weekly Workout Schedule: How to Train Like Cristiano Ronaldo at Home

To truly **train like Cristiano Ronaldo at home**, you need a plan! This step-by-step guide breaks down your week into different focus areas. We'll combine strength, cardio, agility, and flexibility, just like a pro athlete.

Remember to listen to your body and take rest days when you need them. Consistency is more important than doing everything perfectly at first. Let's get started with your **workout schedule for beginners** and beyond!

### Always Start with a Warm-Up!

Before every workout, spend 5-10 minutes warming up your body. This gets your muscles ready and helps prevent injuries. Think of it like getting your car engine ready before a big trip.

*   **Light Cardio:** Jog in place, do some jumping jacks, or high knees.
*   **Dynamic Stretches:** Arm circles, leg swings, torso twists.
*   **Joint Rotations:** Ankle rotations, knee circles, wrist circles.

### Day 1: Strength & Power (Bodyweight & Plyometrics)

Today is all about getting stronger and becoming more explosive! We'll use your own body weight and quick, jumping movements to build power.

#### H3. Bodyweight Exercises for Strength

These exercises use just your body to build muscle. Aim for 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions for each exercise.

1.  **Push-Ups:**
    *   Start on your hands and knees, then straighten your legs behind you.
    *   Lower your chest towards the ground, then push back up.
    *   If it's too hard, do them on your knees or against a wall.
2.  **Squats:**
    *   Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes pointing slightly out.
    *   Imagine sitting back into a chair, lowering your hips until your thighs are parallel to the ground.
    *   Keep your chest up and push through your heels to stand back up.
3.  **Lunges:**
    *   Step forward with one leg, lowering your hips until both knees are bent at a 90-degree angle.
    *   Make sure your front knee is over your ankle, not past your toes.
    *   Push back to the start and alternate legs.
4.  **Plank:**
    *   Get into a push-up position, but rest on your forearms instead of your hands.
    *   Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels, squeezing your abs.
    *   Hold this position for 30-60 seconds. This is a great **core strengthening exercise**.
5.  **Dips (using a chair):**
    *   Sit on the edge of a sturdy chair, hands gripping the edge next to your hips.
    *   Slide your butt off the chair and lower your body by bending your elbows.
    *   Push back up. Make sure the chair is stable!

#### H3. Plyometric Training for Explosiveness

**Plyometric training** makes your muscles super quick and powerful, just like CR7's jumps and sprints. Perform 3 sets of 8-12 reps for each.

1.  **Jump Squats:**
    *   Do a regular squat, but as you come up, explode into a jump.
    *   Land softly back into a squat position.
    *   This builds powerful leg muscles.
2.  **Box Jumps (using a low, sturdy step or object):**
    *   Stand in front of a low, stable box or step.
    *   Jump onto the box with both feet, landing softly.
    *   Step back down carefully. Make sure your landing surface is very stable!
3.  **Broad Jumps:**
    *   Stand with feet shoulder-width apart.
    *   Swing your arms back, squat down slightly, then explode forward into a long jump.
    *   Land softly with bent knees.
4.  **Burpees:**
    *   Start standing, drop into a squat, kick your feet back into a plank.
    *   Do a push-up, jump your feet back to a squat, and then jump straight up.
    *   This is a full-body power move!
    *   *External link for proper form:* You can find great demonstrations for these **bodyweight exercises** and plyometrics on sites like [Bodybuilding.com's exercise guides](https://www.bodybuilding.com/exercises/finder).

### Day 2: Cardio & Agility (Without Equipment)

Today is about speed, stamina, and quick changes of direction, crucial for any footballer. We'll focus on **cardio without equipment** and **football drills at home**.

#### H3. Cardio Without Equipment

These exercises will get your heart pumping and build your endurance. Aim for 30-45 minutes of continuous work, or break it into intervals.

1.  **Jump Rope (15-20 minutes):**
    *   This is fantastic for footwork and endurance. Try different speeds and techniques.
    *   You can get a [Jump Rope](https://www.amazon.com/jump-ropes/dp/B0B5F5G4T2) for quick workouts.
2.  **High Knees:**
    *   Run in place, bringing your knees up towards your chest as high as you can.
    *   Keep your core tight and pump your arms.
3.  **Butt Kicks:**
    *   Run in place, trying to kick your heels back to touch your glutes.
    *   This warms up your hamstrings.
4.  **Shuttle Runs (if you have space):**
    *   Mark two points about 10-20 yards apart.
    *   Sprint from one point to the other, touch the ground, and sprint back. Repeat!
5.  **Stair Sprints (if you have stairs):**
    *   Run up and down a flight of stairs as fast as you can.
    *   Be careful and hold the railing if needed.

#### H3. Football Drills at Home for Agility

You can practice quick feet and ball control even in a small space. These **football drills at home** are key for handling the ball like CR7.

1.  **Cone Dribbling (or use shoes/bottles as cones):**
    *   Set up 4-5 "cones" in a straight line or zigzag pattern.
    *   Dribble a football in and out of them, using both feet.
    *   Focus on quick, light touches, keeping the ball close.
2.  **Figure-8 Dribbling:**
    *   Place two "cones" a few feet apart.
    *   Dribble the ball in a figure-8 pattern around them.
    *   Practice changing direction quickly and smoothly.
3.  **Sole Taps:**
    *   Stand over the ball and lightly tap the top of it with the sole of each foot, alternating.
    *   This improves quickness and control.
4.  **Toe Taps:**
    *   Place one foot on top of the ball.
    *   Quickly switch, tapping the ball with the toe of the other foot.
    *   This helps with balance and quick changes.
5.  **Wall Passing (if you have a sturdy wall):**
    *   Find a strong, empty wall.
    *   Pass the ball against it with different parts of your foot (inside, outside, laces).
    *   Work on receiving the ball smoothly and passing it back quickly.
    *   *External link for football drills:* For more advanced drills, check out resources like [CoachUp's Youth Soccer Drills](https://www.coachup.com/resources/youth-soccer-drills/).

### Day 3: Core & Flexibility

Today is about building a super strong midsection and making your body more flexible. A strong core helps with everything – shooting, running, and staying balanced. Flexibility prevents injuries and makes you move better.

#### H3. Core Strengthening Exercises

A strong core is like the engine of your body. These exercises will help you build one. Aim for 3 sets of 10-15 reps, or hold planks for 30-60 seconds. Do these on your [Yoga Mat](https://www.amazon.com/yoga-mats/dp/B08XJ81N6W).

1.  **Crunches:**
    *   Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat on the floor.
    *   Place your hands lightly behind your head.
    *   Lift your head and shoulders off the ground, squeezing your abs.
2.  **Leg Raises:**
    *   Lie on your back, hands under your lower back for support if needed.
    *   Keep your legs straight and slowly lift them towards the ceiling.
    *   Lower them back down without touching the floor.
3.  **Bicycle Crunches:**
    *   Lie on your back, hands behind your head, legs lifted with knees bent.
    *   Bring one elbow towards the opposite knee while straightening the other leg.
    *   Switch sides in a pedaling motion.
4.  **Side Planks:**
    *   Lie on your side, supporting yourself on one forearm and the side of your foot.
    *   Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
    *   Hold for 30-60 seconds, then switch sides.
5.  **Russian Twists:**
    *   Sit on the floor, knees bent, feet flat. Lean back slightly, keeping your back straight.
    *   Clasp your hands together and twist your torso from side to side, touching the floor beside you.
    *   Lift your feet off the ground for more challenge.
    *   *Affiliate link:* You can add a [Resistance Band](https://www.amazon.com/resistance-bands/dp/B088V34W31) around your ankles for leg raises or for extra resistance during Russian twists.

#### H3. Flexibility and Mobility Work

After your core workout, dedicate 15-20 minutes to stretching. **Flexibility and mobility work** is super important for an athlete like CR7. Hold each stretch for 20-30 seconds, feeling a gentle pull, not pain.

1.  **Hamstring Stretch:**
    *   Sit on the floor, one leg extended, the other bent with your foot against your inner thigh.
    *   Reach towards your toes on the extended leg.
2.  **Quad Stretch:**
    *   Stand tall, grab your ankle with one hand, and gently pull your heel towards your glutes.
    *   Keep your knees together.
3.  **Calf Stretch:**
    *   Stand facing a wall, place your hands on it.
    *   Step one foot back, keeping your heel on the ground, and lean forward.
    *   You should feel the stretch in your calf.
4.  **Hip Flexor Stretch:**
    *   Kneel on one knee, with the other foot flat on the ground in front of you (like a lunge).
    *   Gently push your hips forward until you feel a stretch in the front of your hip.
5.  **Groin Stretch (Butterfly Stretch):**
    *   Sit on the floor, bring the soles of your feet together, and let your knees fall out to the sides.
    *   Gently press your knees towards the floor with your elbows.
6.  **Spinal Twist:**
    *   Lie on your back, arms out to the sides.
    *   Bring one knee up and gently pull it across your body with the opposite hand.
    *   Keep both shoulders on the ground.

### Day 4: Active Recovery or Light Cardio

Today is about letting your muscles recover while still staying active. This could be a light walk, a slow bike ride, or some gentle yoga.

*   **Walk:** Take a leisurely walk outside for 30-45 minutes.
*   **Light Jog:** A very easy jog, just enough to get the blood flowing.
*   **Yoga/Stretching:** Do a longer session of gentle **flexibility and mobility work** using your [Yoga Mat](https://www.amazon.com/yoga-mats/dp/B08XJ81N6W).
*   **Light Cycle:** If you have an [Indoor Exercise Bike](https://www.amazon.com/exercise-bikes/dp/B08M9Y7M7P), do a relaxed 30-45 minute ride.

### Day 5: Repeat Strength & Power (Different Exercises)

Let's hit strength and power again, but with some different moves to challenge your muscles in new ways.

#### H4. Advanced Bodyweight & Dumbbell Exercises

If you have [Adjustable Dumbbells](https://www.amazon.com/adjustable-dumbbells/dp/B085V5J3M7) or a [Pull-Up Bar](https://www.amazon.com/pull-up-bars/dp/B08M3Q7T2S), this is where you can use them. If not, stick to bodyweight and increase your reps or sets! Aim for 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions.

1.  **Pull-Ups/Chin-Ups:**
    *   Using your pull-up bar, hang with hands gripping the bar.
    *   Pull your body up until your chin is over the bar.
    *   If you can't do full pull-ups, try negatives (jump up and slowly lower yourself) or use a resistance band for assistance.
2.  **Dumbbell Rows (using one dumbbell and a chair):**
    *   Place one knee and hand on a chair, holding a dumbbell in the other hand.
    *   Pull the dumbbell up towards your chest, squeezing your back muscles.
    *   Lower slowly and repeat.
3.  **Goblet Squats (with a dumbbell):**
    *   Hold one dumbbell vertically against your chest.
    *   Perform a squat, keeping your chest up and elbows inside your knees.
4.  **Glute Bridges:**
    *   Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat.
    *   Lift your hips off the ground until your body forms a straight line from knees to shoulders.
    *   Squeeze your glutes at the top. You can place a [Resistance Band](https://www.amazon.com/resistance-bands/dp/B088V34W31) around your knees for added challenge.
5.  **Pike Push-Ups:**
    *   Start in a downward dog yoga pose, with your hips high.
    *   Bend your elbows, lowering the top of your head towards the floor.
    *   This targets your shoulders.

#### H4. More Plyometric Training

Keep building that explosive power! 3 sets of 8-12 reps.

1.  **Split Squat Jumps:**
    *   Start in a lunge position.
    *   Explode upwards, switching your legs in mid-air, and land in a lunge with the opposite leg forward.
2.  **Skater Jumps:**
    *   Jump sideways from one foot to the other, bringing the trailing leg behind you.
    *   Focus on distance and quickness.
3.  **High Box Jumps (if you have a higher, sturdy box):**
    *   If you've mastered low box jumps, try a slightly higher one. Always prioritize safety!

### Day 6: Repeat Cardio & Agility (New Drills)

Another day to get your heart pumping and improve your football skills.

#### H4. Interval Cardio Blast

Today, let's try some interval training for your **cardio without equipment**. Work hard for a short time, then rest.

*   **Sprint/Jog Intervals:** Sprint as fast as you can for 30 seconds, then jog or walk slowly for 60 seconds. Repeat 10-15 times.
*   **Jump Rope Intervals:** Jump rope fast for 45 seconds, rest for 15 seconds. Repeat for 15-20 minutes. Your [Jump Rope](https://www.amazon.com/jump-ropes/dp/B0B5F5G4T2) is perfect for this.
*   **Burpee Tabata:** Do burpees for 20 seconds, rest for 10 seconds. Repeat 8 times (4 minutes). This is a killer!

#### H4. Advanced Football Drills at Home

Let's step up your **football drills at home** with some more challenging moves.

1.  **Tick-Tocks (inside of foot taps):**
    *   Stand over the ball. Quickly tap the ball from side to side using the inside of your foot, alternating feet.
    *   Keep your head up!
2.  **Roll-Overs (sole rolls):**
    *   Place the sole of your foot on top of the ball.
    *   Roll the ball across your body to the other foot.
    *   This is great for changing direction.
3.  **Juggling:**
    *   Try to keep the ball in the air using your feet, thighs, and head.
    *   Start with just one touch, then try for two, then more.
    *   This dramatically improves ball control and touch.
4.  **Close Control Dribbling (small space):**
    *   Mark out a tiny square (e.g., 5x5 feet).
    *   Dribble within that square, taking tiny, quick touches, making sure the ball never leaves the area.
    *   This teaches incredible ball mastery in tight spaces.

### Day 7: Rest and Recover

Today is a complete rest day! Your muscles need time to repair and grow stronger. This is just as important as the workouts themselves.

You can do some very gentle stretching or just relax. Your body will thank you!

## Your Weekly Workout Schedule: How to Train Like Cristiano Ronaldo at Home

*   **Light Cardio:** Jog in place, do some jumping jacks, or high knees.

### Day 1: Strength & Power (Bodyweight & Plyometrics)

Today is all about getting stronger and becoming more explosive! We'll use your own body weight and quick, jumping movements to build power.

#### Bodyweight Exercises for Strength

These exercises use just your body to build muscle. Aim for 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions for each exercise.

1.  **Push-Ups:**
    *   Start on your hands and knees, then straighten your legs behind you.
    *   Lower your chest towards the ground, then push back up.
    *   If it's too hard, do them on your knees or against a wall.
2.  **Squats:**
    *   Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes pointing slightly out.
    *   Imagine sitting back into a chair, lowering your hips until your thighs are parallel to the ground.
    *   Keep your chest up and push through your heels to stand back up.
3.  **Lunges:**
    *   Step forward with one leg, lowering your hips until both knees are bent at a 90-degree angle.
    *   Make sure your front knee is over your ankle, not past your toes.
    *   Push back to the start and alternate legs.
4.  **Plank:**
    *   Get into a push-up position, but rest on your forearms instead of your hands.
    *   Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels, squeezing your abs.
    *   Hold this position for 30-60 seconds. This is a great **core strengthening exercise**.
5.  **Dips (using a chair):**
    *   Sit on the edge of a sturdy chair, hands gripping the edge next to your hips.
    *   Slide your butt off the chair and lower your body by bending your elbows.
    *   Push back up. Make sure the chair is stable!

#### Plyometric Training for Explosiveness

**Plyometric training** makes your muscles super quick and powerful, just like CR7's jumps and sprints. Perform 3 sets of 8-12 reps for each.

1.  **Jump Squats:**
    *   Do a regular squat, but as you come up, explode into a jump.
    *   Land softly back into a squat position.
    *   This builds powerful leg muscles.
2.  **Box Jumps (using a low, sturdy step or object):**
    *   Stand in front of a low, stable box or step.
    *   Jump onto the box with both feet, landing softly.
    *   Step back down carefully. Make sure your landing surface is very stable!
3.  **Broad Jumps:**
    *   Stand with feet shoulder-width apart.
    *   Swing your arms back, squat down slightly, then explode forward into a long jump.
    *   Land softly with bent knees.
4.  **Burpees:**
    *   Start standing, drop into a squat, kick your feet back into a plank.
    *   Do a push-up, jump your feet back to a squat, and then jump straight up.
    *   This is a full-body power move!
    *   *External link for proper form:* You can find great demonstrations for these **bodyweight exercises** and plyometrics on sites like [Bodybuilding.com's exercise guides](https://www.bodybuilding.com/exercises/finder).

### Day 2: Cardio & Agility (Without Equipment)

Today is about speed, stamina, and quick changes of direction, crucial for any footballer. We'll focus on **cardio without equipment** and **football drills at home**.

#### Cardio Without Equipment

These exercises will get your heart pumping and build your endurance. Aim for 30-45 minutes of continuous work, or break it into intervals.

1.  **Jump Rope (15-20 minutes):**
    *   This is fantastic for footwork and endurance. Try different speeds and techniques.
    *   You can get a [Jump Rope](https://www.amazon.com/jump-ropes/dp/B0B5F5G4T2) for quick workouts.
2.  **High Knees:**
    *   Run in place, bringing your knees up towards your chest as high as you can.
    *   Keep your core tight and pump your arms.
3.  **Butt Kicks:**
    *   Run in place, trying to kick your heels back to touch your glutes.
    *   This warms up your hamstrings.
4.  **Shuttle Runs (if you have space):**
    *   Mark two points about 10-20 yards apart.
    *   Sprint from one point to the other, touch the ground, and sprint back. Repeat!
5.  **Stair Sprints (if you have stairs):**
    *   Run up and down a flight of stairs as fast as you can.
    *   Be careful and hold the railing if needed.

#### Football Drills at Home for Agility

You can practice quick feet and ball control even in a small space. These **football drills at home** are key for handling the ball like CR7.

1.  **Cone Dribbling (or use shoes/bottles as cones):**
    *   Set up 4-5 "cones" in a straight line or zigzag pattern.
    *   Dribble a football in and out of them, using both feet.
    *   Focus on quick, light touches, keeping the ball close.
2.  **Figure-8 Dribbling:**
    *   Place two "cones" a few feet apart.
    *   Dribble the ball in a figure-8 pattern around them.
    *   Practice changing direction quickly and smoothly.
3.  **Sole Taps:**
    *   Stand over the ball and lightly tap the top of it with the sole of each foot, alternating.
    *   This improves quickness and control.
4.  **Toe Taps:**
    *   Place one foot on top of the ball.
    *   Quickly switch, tapping the ball with the toe of the other foot.
    *   This helps with balance and quick changes.
5.  **Wall Passing (if you have a sturdy wall):**
    *   Find a strong, empty wall.
    *   Pass the ball against it with different parts of your foot (inside, outside, laces).
    *   Work on receiving the ball smoothly and passing it back quickly.
    *   *External link for football drills:* For more advanced drills, check out resources like [CoachUp's Youth Soccer Drills](https://www.coachup.com/resources/youth-soccer-drills/).

### Day 3: Core & Flexibility

Today is about building a super strong midsection and making your body more flexible. A strong core helps with everything – shooting, running, and staying balanced. Flexibility prevents injuries and makes you move better.

#### Core Strengthening Exercises

A strong core is like the engine of your body. These exercises will help you build one. Aim for 3 sets of 10-15 reps, or hold planks for 30-60 seconds. Do these on your [Yoga Mat](https://www.amazon.com/yoga-mats/dp/B08XJ81N6W).

1.  **Crunches:**
    *   Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat on the floor.
    *   Place your hands lightly behind your head.
    *   Lift your head and shoulders off the ground, squeezing your abs.
2.  **Leg Raises:**
    *   Lie on your back, hands under your lower back for support if needed.
    *   Keep your legs straight and slowly lift them towards the ceiling.
    *   Lower them back down without touching the floor.
3.  **Bicycle Crunches:**
    *   Lie on your back, hands behind your head, legs lifted with knees bent.
    *   Bring one elbow towards the opposite knee while straightening the other leg.
    *   Switch sides in a pedaling motion.
4.  **Side Planks:**
    *   Lie on your side, supporting yourself on one forearm and the side of your foot.
    *   Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
    *   Hold for 30-60 seconds, then switch sides.
5.  **Russian Twists:**
    *   Sit on the floor, knees bent, feet flat. Lean back slightly, keeping your back straight.
    *   Clasp your hands together and twist your torso from side to side, touching the floor beside you.
    *   Lift your feet off the ground for more challenge.
    *   *Affiliate link:* You can add a [Resistance Band](https://www.amazon.com/resistance-bands/dp/B088V34W31) around your ankles for leg raises or for extra resistance during Russian twists.

#### Flexibility and Mobility Work

After your core workout, dedicate 15-20 minutes to stretching. **Flexibility and mobility work** is super important for an athlete like CR7. Hold each stretch for 20-30 seconds, feeling a gentle pull, not pain.

1.  **Hamstring Stretch:**
    *   Sit on the floor, one leg extended, the other bent with your foot against your inner thigh.
    *   Reach towards your toes on the extended leg.
2.  **Quad Stretch:**
    *   Stand tall, grab your ankle with one hand, and gently pull your heel towards your glutes.
    *   Keep your knees together.
3.  **Calf Stretch:**
    *   Stand facing a wall, place your hands on it.
    *   Step one foot back, keeping your heel on the ground, and lean forward.
    *   You should feel the stretch in your calf.
4.  **Hip Flexor Stretch:**
    *   Kneel on one knee, with the other foot flat on the ground in front of you (like a lunge).
    *   Gently push your hips forward until you feel a stretch in the front of your hip.
5.  **Groin Stretch (Butterfly Stretch):**
    *   Sit on the floor, bring the soles of your feet together, and let your knees fall out to the sides.
    *   Gently press your knees towards the floor with your elbows.
6.  **Spinal Twist:**
    *   Lie on your back, arms out to the sides.
    *   Bring one knee up and gently pull it across your body with the opposite hand.
    *   Keep both shoulders on the ground.

#### Advanced Bodyweight & Dumbbell Exercises

If you have [Adjustable Dumbbells](https://www.amazon.com/adjustable-dumbbells/dp/B085V5J3M7) or a [Pull-Up Bar](https://www.amazon.com/pull-up-bars/dp/B08M3Q7T2S), this is where you can use them. If not, stick to bodyweight and increase your reps or sets! Aim for 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions.

1.  **Pull-Ups/Chin-Ups:**
    *   Using your pull-up bar, hang with hands gripping the bar.
    *   Pull your body up until your chin is over the bar.
    *   If you can't do full pull-ups, try negatives (jump up and slowly lower yourself) or use a resistance band for assistance.
2.  **Dumbbell Rows (using one dumbbell and a chair):**
    *   Place one knee and hand on a chair, holding a dumbbell in the other hand.
    *   Pull the dumbbell up towards your chest, squeezing your back muscles.
    *   Lower slowly and repeat.
3.  **Goblet Squats (with a dumbbell):**
    *   Hold one dumbbell vertically against your chest.
    *   Perform a squat, keeping your chest up and elbows inside your knees.
4.  **Glute Bridges:**
    *   Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat.
    *   Lift your hips off the ground until your body forms a straight line from knees to shoulders.
    *   Squeeze your glutes at the top. You can place a [Resistance Band](https://www.amazon.com/resistance-bands/dp/B088V34W31) around your knees for added challenge.
5.  **Pike Push-Ups:**
    *   Start in a downward dog yoga pose, with your hips high.
    *   Bend your elbows, lowering the top of your head towards the floor.
    *   This targets your shoulders.

#### More Plyometric Training

Keep building that explosive power! 3 sets of 8-12 reps.

1.  **Split Squat Jumps:**

    *   Explode upwards, switching your legs in mid-air, and land in a lunge with the opposite leg forward.
2.  **Skater Jumps:**
    *   Jump sideways from one foot to the other, bringing the trailing leg behind you.
    *   Focus on distance and quickness.
3.  **High Box Jumps (if you have a higher, sturdy box):**
    *   If you've mastered low box jumps, try a slightly higher one. Always prioritize safety!

*   **Burpee Tabata:** Do burpees for 20 seconds, rest for 10 seconds. Repeat 8 times (4 minutes). This is a killer!

#### Advanced Football Drills at Home

Let's step up your **football drills at home** with some more challenging moves.

1.  **Tick-Tocks (inside of foot taps):**
    *   Stand over the ball. Quickly tap the ball from side to side using the inside of your foot, alternating feet.
    *   Keep your head up!
2.  **Roll-Overs (sole rolls):**
    *   Place the sole of your foot on top of the ball.
    *   Roll the ball across your body to the other foot.
    *   This is great for changing direction.
3.  **Juggling:**
    *   Try to keep the ball in the air using your feet, thighs, and head.
    *   Start with just one touch, then try for two, then more.
    *   This dramatically improves ball control and touch.
4.  **Close Control Dribbling (small space):**
    *   Mark out a tiny square (e.g., 5x5 feet).
    *   Dribble within that square, taking tiny, quick touches, making sure the ball never leaves the area.
    *   This teaches incredible ball mastery in tight spaces.
